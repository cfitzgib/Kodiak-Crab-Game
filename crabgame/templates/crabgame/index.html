About Crab n'Click

The Kodiak Lab is part of NOAAâ€™s Shellfish Assessment Program, which seeks to assess the distribution and abundance of commercially important crab and groundfish resources in the eastern Bering Sea. Each year, the Kodiak Lab goes out to sea and brings back 400-800 crabs. One way to determine if a crab is going to mate each year is to analyze its oocytes - the female reproductive cells before they are fertilized. With global warming and climate change, the reproductive systems of these snow crabs have become inconsistent. Help the Kodiak Lab researchers analyze these crab's oocytes so they can locate productive fishing grounds and help Crab Fisheries Management regulate takes to improve the overall viability of future crab stocks.

Learn more about the Kodiak Lab or the Shellfish Assessment Program.
